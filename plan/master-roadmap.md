# Master Teaching Roadmap: Mission IIT

This plan is designed to synchronize Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics so that students never encounter a concept in one subject without having the necessary foundation from another.

## Phase 1: The Language of Science (Weeks 1-4)
*Focus: Tools and basic counting.*

| Subject | Topic | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maths** | Sets, Relations & Basic Algebra | Sets are the foundation of all mathematical definitions. |
| **Physics** | Units, Dimensions & Errors | Teaches students how to measure and the "sanity check" of units. |
| **Chemistry** | Mole Concept & Stoichiometry | The fundamental "counting" unit in chemistry. |

**Interdisciplinary Connection:** The precision learned in Physics (Errors) applies to the calculations in Chemistry (Stoichiometry).

---

## Phase 2: Building Blocks & 1D Logic (Weeks 5-8)
*Focus: Linear thinking and atomic reality.*

| Subject | Topic | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maths** | Quadratic Equations & Basic Trig | Tools needed for resolving vectors and solving kinematics. |
| **Physics** | Kinematics (1D) | Introduces rate of change (Velocity/Acceleration). |
| **Chemistry** | Atomic Structure | Moves from "counting" to the physical structure of matter. |

**Interdisciplinary Connection:** Bohr’s model in Chemistry requires basic Electrostatic logic (covered informally) and the centripetal force concept from Physics.

---

## Phase 3: Forces & Vector Spaces (Weeks 9-14)
*Focus: Vectors, Functions, and Periodic Trends.*

| Subject | Topic | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maths** | Functions & Basic Calculus | **Critical:** Must understand $dy/dx$ and $\int$ for Physics. |
| **Physics** | Kinematics (2D) & NLM | Vectors are used to decompose forces and motions. |
| **Chemistry** | Periodic Table & Bonding | Understanding how forces (Physics) create molecules. |

**Interdisciplinary Connection:** Differentiation in Maths is used to find instantaneous velocity in Physics. Coulombic forces in Chemistry are explained via Physics laws.

---

## Phase 4: Energy, Work & States (Weeks 15-20)
*Focus: Conservation laws and macroscopic behavior.*

| Subject | Topic | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maths** | Sequences & Series | Patterns and summations used in Thermodynamics and Statistics. |
| **Physics** | Work, Power, Energy & Friction | The concept of Energy Conservation. |
| **Chemistry** | Gaseous State & Thermodynamics | Macroscopic behavior of energy in chemical systems. |

**Interdisciplinary Connection:** Thermodynamics is the bridge where Physics and Chemistry merge. Kinetic Theory of Gases is common to both.

---

## Phase 5: Rotations & Coordinate Geometry (Weeks 21-28)
*Focus: Complex systems and spatial reasoning.*

| Subject | Topic | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maths** | Straight Lines & Circles | Coordinate geometry tools for complex Physics problems. |
| **Physics** | Rotational Mechanics | The peak of Class 11 Physics. High complexity. |
| **Chemistry** | Equilibrium (Chemical & Ionic) | Balancing opposing "forces" in chemical reactions. |

**Interdisciplinary Connection:** Moments of inertia (Physics) and Circle properties (Maths) go hand-in-hand. Equilibrium requires solid algebraic manipulation (Maths).

---

## Phase 6: Finishing Class 11 & Bridge to 12
*Focus: Waves, Conics, and Organic Foundations.*

| Subject | Topic | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maths** | Conic Sections | Advanced spatial visualization. |
| **Physics** | Waves & SHM | Cyclic motion and periodic functions. |
| **Chemistry** | Basic Organic Chemistry | The start of the Organic journey. |

---

## How to use this plan
1. **Vertical Alignment:** Follow the phases. Don't move to Phase 3 until Phase 2 is solid.
2. **Horizontal Alignment:** Try to teach the "connected" topics in the same week. For example, explain "Slope of a Graph" in Maths at the same time you teach "Velocity-Time Graphs" in Physics.

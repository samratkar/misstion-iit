# Question-Based Pedagogy: Mole Concept Basics

## Objective
Understand the mole as a counting unit and its relation to mass and volume.

---

### Question 1: The Scale of Atoms
The mass of one Carbon-12 atom is approximately $1.99 \times 10^{-23}$ grams.
- **Q1.1:** Why is it impractical to use "grams" for individual atoms in a lab?
- **Q1.2:** If we define a "bundle" of atoms such that the mass of the bundle in grams is numerically equal to the atomic mass in amu (12.00), how many atoms must be in that bundle?

**Concept Takeaway:** Avogadro's Number ($N_A \approx 6.022 \times 10^{23}$) is the bridge between the microscopic (amu) and macroscopic (grams).

---

### Question 2: Counting by Weighing
You have 36 grams of Water ($H_2O$). (Atomic masses: $H=1, O=16$)
- **Q2.1:** What is the molar mass of $H_2O$?
- **Q2.2:** How many moles of water molecules do you have?
- **Q2.3:** How many total atoms (Hydrogen + Oxygen) are present in this sample?

**Concept Takeaway:** Moles ($n$) = Mass ($m$) / Molar Mass ($M$).

---

### Question 3: The Gaseous State
At STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure), 1 mole of any ideal gas occupies 22.4 Liters.
- **Q3.1:** What is the volume occupied by 4.4 grams of $CO_2$ at STP? ($C=12, O=16$)
- **Q3.2:** If the pressure is doubled while keeping temperature constant, what happens to the volume of 1 mole of gas?

**Concept Takeaway:** Molar Volume at STP is a constant for ideal gases.

---

### Question 4: Concentration in Solutions
You dissolve 40 grams of $NaOH$ in enough water to make 500 mL of solution. ($Na=23, O=16, H=1$)
- **Q4.1:** How many moles of $NaOH$ are present?
- **Q4.2:** What is the Molarity (moles per liter) of the solution?

**Concept Takeaway:** $Molarity (M) = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{Volume of solution in Liters}}$.

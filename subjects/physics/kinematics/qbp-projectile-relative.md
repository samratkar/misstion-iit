# Question-Based Pedagogy: 2D Motion (Projectile & Relative)

## Objective
Analyze motion in two dimensions by decomposing vectors and understanding relative frames.

---

### Question 1: Independence of Horizontal and Vertical Motion
A ball is thrown horizontally from a cliff of height $H$ with velocity $u$. Simultaneously, another ball is dropped from the same height.
- **Q1.1:** Which ball hits the ground first? (Assume no air resistance).
- **Q1.2:** What is the horizontal distance (range) covered by the first ball when it hits the ground?
- **Q1.3:** Does the horizontal velocity change during the flight? Why?

**Concept Takeaway:** $X$ and $Y$ motions are independent. Gravity only affects vertical velocity ($a_y = -g, a_x = 0$).

---

### Question 2: The Projectile Trajectory
A projectile is fired with speed $u$ at an angle $\theta$ to the horizontal.
- **Q2.1:** Express the horizontal and vertical components of velocity at any time $t$.
- **Q2.2:** Derive the expression for Time of Flight ($T$) and Maximum Height ($H_{max}$).
- **Q2.3:** Show that for a fixed $u$, the range is same for angles $\theta$ and $(90^\circ - \theta)$.

**Concept Takeaway:** Range $R = \frac{u^2 \sin 2\theta}{g}$. Maximum range occurs at $45^\circ$.

---

### Question 3: Relative Motion in 2D (Rain-Man Problems)
Rain is falling vertically at $3 \text{ km/h}$. A man walks horizontally at $4 \text{ km/h}$.
- **Q3.1:** What is the velocity of rain *relative to the man* ($\vec{v}_{RM}$)?
- **Q3.2:** At what angle should the man hold his umbrella to stay dry?
- **Q3.3:** If the man starts running faster, does the angle of the umbrella increase or decrease?

**Concept Takeaway:** $\vec{v}_{AB} = \vec{v}_A - \vec{v}_B$. Vectors must be subtracted component-wise.

---

### Question 4: River-Boat Problems
A boat can travel at $v_{br}$ in still water. The river flows at $v_r$. The width of the river is $d$.
- **Q3.1:** To cross the river in the "shortest time", in which direction should the boat point?
- **Q3.2:** To cross the river via the "shortest path" (straight across), what condition must be met by $v_{br}$ and $v_r$?
- **Q3.3:** Calculate the "drift" if the boat points perpendicular to the current.

**Concept Takeaway:** Shortest time: point perpendicular to bank ($t = d/v_{br}$). Shortest path: point upstream such that horizontal resultant is zero.
